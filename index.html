Philosophy

On teaching mathematics

Nobody is "bad at maths." There are only missing steps — and every missing step can be found, named, and rebuilt.

I teach GCSE mathematics the way the subject actually works: one line at a time, each line justified by the one before it. A student who memorises a method owns it until the exam changes the question. A student who understands the method owns it for good.

Show your working

My approach comes from the classroom, not a script. I trained through a PGCE across two Sheffield-area schools and taught for a year in a full mathematics department — planning lessons, marking to exam-board standards, and learning exactly where students lose marks and why.

In a one-to-one session that experience is concentrated on a single student: find the gap, close it properly, and build the confidence that comes from getting it right for a reason.
